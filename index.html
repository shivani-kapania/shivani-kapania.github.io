Hi! 🤠 I am a Pre-Doctoral Researcher with the Google Research India and the People+AI Research (PAIR) team focusing on research situated at the intersection of HCI and responsible ML. I draw on theories and methods from HCI towards two goals: (1) better understanding the dataset practices and processes in the ML pipeline, and (2) calibrating user trust and moving towards greater end-user agency in interacting with machine learning systems.

I have been fortunate to work with and learn from many of my scholarly heroes. I currently work with Ding Wang and Alex Taylor where we used mixed-methods research to interrogate the practices of dataset production, particularly as they relate to the diversity of data annotators. Until recently, I worked with Nithya Sambasivan, on human-data interaction across the ML pipeline.

Before joining Google Research, I worked as a Research Associate at IIIT Delhi with Pushpendra Singh, and to whom I am grateful for introducing me to HCI as a discipline. Together with my colleagues Anupriya Tuli and Ayushi Srivastava, we developed a framework containing actionable guidelines for designing mobile applications for low-literacy users. Previously, I spent the summer of 2018 as an intern with the Regenerative Medicine and Stem Cells lab, working with Subha Narayan Rath on automated cancer cell counting using Computer Vision.

I graduated with a B.Tech in Computer Science from Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University in Delhi in 2019. I am passionate about teaching, and spent a lot of time as a volunteering to tutor science and math to middle school students with the Aashmaan Foundation. I have also worked with UpGrad as an instructor of Data Science using Python for university students. Outside of work, I love reading historical fiction and trying new recipes. For more details, check out my CV or contact me by email!
